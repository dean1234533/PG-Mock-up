Privacy Policy

We take your privacy seriously. This policy explains what data we collect, how we use it, and your rights under UK data protection law.

Last updated: 1 January 2026  |  Da Gym, Registered in England & Wales,

1. Who We Are

Da Gym ("Da Gym", "we", "us", "our") is the data controller responsible for your personal data. Our registered office is at Stratford, London, E15.

Our Data Protection Officer can be contacted at: deanburt1308@gmail.com

2. What Data We Collect

We collect and process the following categories of personal data:

Category Examples
Identity data Name, date of birth, gender
Contact data Email address, phone number, home address
Financial data Bank account details, payment card information (processed securely via our payment provider)
Health data PAR-Q responses, injury or medical information you choose to share
Usage data Gym visits, class bookings, access times, equipment use patterns
Biometric data Fingerprint or facial recognition data used for gym access (where enabled)
Technical data IP address, browser type, device identifiers, app usage data
Marketing preferences Your communication and marketing opt-in/opt-out choices

3. How We Use Your Data

We use your personal data for the following purposes:

  • Membership administration — to create and manage your account, process payments, and provide gym access.
  • Service delivery — to enable class bookings, manage PT appointments, and send booking confirmations.
  • Safety and health — to assess health and safety risks and ensure your wellbeing in our gyms.
  • Communications — to send you important account information, service updates, and (where you have consented) marketing communications.
  • Improvement of services — to analyse usage patterns, conduct research, and improve our facilities and app.
  • Legal compliance — to comply with applicable laws and regulations, including HMRC reporting obligations.
  • Security — to protect our members, staff, and property through CCTV monitoring and access logs.

4. Legal Basis for Processing

We rely on the following lawful bases under the UK GDPR:

  • Contract performance — processing necessary to provide your membership and deliver our services.
  • Legitimate interests — improving our services, preventing fraud, and ensuring gym security.
  • Legal obligation — compliance with tax, employment, and health & safety laws.
  • Consent — marketing communications and biometric data processing (you may withdraw consent at any time).
  • Vital interests — in medical emergencies where we need to share your health data with emergency services.

5. Sharing Your Data

We may share your personal data with:

  • Payment processors — to handle your Direct Debit and card payments securely.
  • IT and software providers — who support our app, website, and gym management systems.
  • Personal trainers — if you book a session with a PT at our gym, they will receive your name and booking details.
  • Analytics partners — to help us understand how our website and app are used (data is anonymised where possible).
  • Legal authorities — where required by law or to protect the safety of our members and staff.

We do not sell your personal data to third parties.

6. International Transfers

Some of our service providers are based outside the UK. Where we transfer data internationally, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, such as Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO).

7. Data Retention

We retain your personal data for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes outlined in this policy, and in accordance with our legal obligations:

  • Active membership data: for the duration of your membership plus 7 years.
  • Financial records: 7 years from the end of the tax year (HMRC requirement).
  • Health questionnaire data: 3 years from last gym visit.
  • CCTV footage: up to 31 days (unless required for an investigation).
  • Marketing data: until you unsubscribe or withdraw consent.

8. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access — to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification — to ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure — to ask us to delete your data in certain circumstances ("right to be forgotten").
  • Right to restrict processing — to ask us to pause processing of your data.
  • Right to data portability — to receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to object — to object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing.
  • Rights relating to automated decisions — not to be subject to solely automated decisions that significantly affect you.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at deanburt1308@gmail.com. We will respond within 30 days. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk.

9. Cookies & Tracking

We use cookies and similar technologies on our website and app to:

  • Essential cookies — to make our website work correctly (e.g., keeping you logged in, remembering your cart).
  • Analytics cookies — to understand how visitors use our site (we use Google Analytics with IP anonymisation).
  • Marketing cookies — to show you relevant ads on third-party platforms (only with your consent).
  • Preference cookies — to remember your settings and preferences.

You can control cookies through your browser settings at any time. Disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website. When you first visit our site, we will ask for your consent to set non-essential cookies.

10. Security

We take the security of your personal data seriously and have implemented appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect it against accidental loss, destruction, alteration, disclosure, or unauthorised access. Payment card data is processed using industry-standard PCI DSS compliant systems.

In the event of a data breach that is likely to affect your rights and freedoms, we will notify you and the ICO in accordance with our legal obligations.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we make significant changes, we will notify you by email or by posting a prominent notice on our website. The date at the top of this policy indicates when it was last revised.

12. Contact Us

For any questions, concerns, or to exercise your data rights, please contact our Data Protection Officer:
